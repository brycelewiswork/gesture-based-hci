Hold each pose steady, then click. Open + fist set your finger range (0 = fist, 1 = open); spread + together set your splay range. This makes the finger bars read *your* hand instead of hard-coded guesses.

Data capture

Idle.

Logs the raw 21-point hand plus every derived feature, each frame. Hold one pose and move your hand through as much of the space as you can — roll, pitch, yaw, near, far, high, low. Stop, and the file path appears.

Tuning

Gestures

—

A motion you perform — a flick, a spread, a wrist rotation, a push toward the camera. Just repeat it at your own pace; it keeps going until the signal is strong, then stops itself (or hit Done). Reps that look like each other are kept; your return-to-start and stray moves are dropped automatically by similarity, so you don't have to control your timing. It learns the whole hand over time — pose, rotation, position, and depth.

A shape you hold, still — for gestures that aren't a movement at all. Hold it and it captures the whole hand, exactly as you make it. Only needed if you want a hold-to-fire gesture.

Recognition test

—

Detection run: say how many times you'll do it, hit Start, perform it that many times at your own pace, then Stop. It counts what fires — fewer than you did = misses, more = something you didn't intend (a return, a stray) is also matching. Quiet check: hit it, then use your hand normally without the gesture — reach, talk with your hands, drop back to rest — anything that fires is a false positive. The attempt count comes from you; it's never guessed from the video.
